Emergency Care

When to Seek Emergency Care

Emergency care is for critical illnesses and injuries that require immediate medical attention. When in doubt, always err on the side of caution and seek help. Here are some signs you may need emergency care:

  • Severe chest pain, shortness of breath, or difficulty breathing
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg
  • Severe pain in the head, neck, or abdomen
  • Confusion or changes in mental alertness
  • Seizures
  • Severe allergic reactions
  • Heavy bleeding that won't stop
  • Burns
  • Broken bones
  • Any situation where you believe your life or someone else's is in danger

Remember: This list is not exhaustive. If you experience any concerning symptoms, call emergency services or proceed to the nearest emergency department.

What to Expect at the Emergency Department

If you need to go to the emergency department, here's a general idea of what to expect:

  • You will be triaged by a nurse to determine the severity of your condition.
  • You may have to wait for treatment depending on how busy the department is.
  • You will be seen by a doctor or other healthcare professional who will examine you and order any necessary tests.
  • You may be treated and released home, admitted to the hospital for further care, or referred to another specialist.
